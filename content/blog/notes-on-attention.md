A short list, not because I've figured anything out, but because writing it down helps.

## Three things I've stopped

**Reading email before I've written anything.** This is the one that mattered most. The day shapes itself around whatever I look at first. If I look at email, the day becomes other people's. If I look at a manuscript, it becomes mine. The fix was almost laughably simple: I moved the email app off my phone's home screen and put a notebook on my desk.

**Trying to read everything.** I used to feel guilty about books I hadn't gotten to. The unread pile on the floor. The list of "important" novels I was supposed to know. Now I read what I want to read. The list has not punished me for ignoring it.

**Checking the news at night.** Nothing useful happens between 9 PM and 7 AM. If anything important breaks, I'll hear about it. The cost of _not_ checking is a couple hours of unnecessary worry. The savings: better sleep, a quieter Sunday morning.

## One thing I started

**Walking without a podcast.** Sounds trivial. It isn't. The walks where I don't fill the silence are the ones where the next paragraph shows up. The podcasts are good — I miss them — but the silence is better.

That's the whole list. I'll revisit in a year.
